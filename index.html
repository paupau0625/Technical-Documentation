Introduction

Motorcycles are one of the most efficient vehicles for travel, offering speed, flexibility, and low fuel consumption.

Understanding how motorcycles work makes maintenance easier and helps riders troubleshoot common issues.

Basic motorcycle knowledge can help prevent breakdowns and reduce repair costs.

Basics

The essential parts of a motorcycle include the engine, transmission, brakes, tires, and electrical system.

These components work together to make the motorcycle accelerate, stop, and remain stable.

Engine = The heart of the motorcycle

The engine burns fuel to create power that rotates the wheels.

Motorcycle Types

Each type varies in comfort, speed, power, and performance.

Sportbike = High-speed performance motorcycle

Some motorcycles are designed for daily commuting, while others are built for long-distance touring or off-road riding.

These include ignition, fuel combustion, steering, braking, and suspension movement.

Modern motorcycles are equipped with advanced features such as ABS, traction control, ride modes, and electric motors.
